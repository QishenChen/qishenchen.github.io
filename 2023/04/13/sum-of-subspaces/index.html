Proposition:

Let $S$ be a linear space and $S_{1}$ and $S_{2}$ two subspaces of $S$. Then, the sum $S_{1}+S_{2}$ is a subspace of $S$
Proof:

Randomly choose two vectors $s,t \in S_{1},S_{2}$. By the definition of the sum of two subspaces, there exist vectors $s_{1},t_{1} \in S_{1} $ and $s_{2},t_{2} \in S_{2}$ such that
$$
s=s_{1}+s_{2}
$$
and $t= t_{1}+t_{2}$.

Consider a linear combination of $s$ and $t$ with scalar coefficient $\sigma $ and $\gamma$
$$
\sigma s+ \gamma t= \sigma s_{1}+ \sigma s_{2} + \gamma t_{1}+ \gamma t_{2}
$$

$$
\sigma s_{1}+ \gamma t_{1} \in S_{1}
$$

and
$$
\sigma s_{2}+ \gamma t_{2} \in S_{2}
$$
Therefore, the linear combination $\sigma s+ \gamma t$ can be written as a sum of a vector of $S_{1}$

and a vector of $S_{2}$. Thus ,
$$
\sigma s+ \gamma t \in S_{1}+S_{2}
$$
In other words, $S_{1}+S_{2}$ is a linear subspace.

Definition(direct sum of subspaces):

Let $S$ be a linear space. Let $S_{1},\dots,S_{n}$ be subspaces of $S$. The sum $s_{1}+ \dots + s_{n}$ is called direct sum and is donated by
$$
s_{1} \oplus s_{2} \dots s_{n}
$$
or
$$
\bigoplus_{i=1}^{n}s_{i}
$$
if and only if $s_{1}+ \dots + s_{n}$ are linearly independent whenever $s_{j} \in S_{j}$ and $s_{j} \ne 0$ for $j=1, \dots , n$
