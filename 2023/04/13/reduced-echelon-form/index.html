Definition (row echelon form):

  1. all its non-zero rows have an entry, called the pivot, that is non-zero and has only zero entries below it and to its left.
  2. zero-rows (if there are any) are below the non-zero rows.

Definition(reduced row echelon form):

A matrix is in reduced row echelon form if and only if it is in a row reduced echelon form, all its pivots are equal to 1 and the pivots are the only non-zero entries of the basic columns.

How to solve a system in reduced echelon form

Consider a linear form
$$
Ax=b
$$
where $A$ is a $K \times L$ matrix of coefficients, $x$ is an $L \times 1$ vector of unknowns, and $b$ is a $K \times 1$ vector of constants.

The system is said to be in reduced row echelon form if the matrix $A$ is in reduced row echelon form.

Since the reduced echelon form is a special case of the echelon form, the conditions for the existence of a solution of a system in the form apply, and we can use the back-substitution algorithm to solve the problem.

If an unknown multiplies a basic column, it is called a basic variable. Otherwise, if it corresponds to a non-basic column, it is called a non-basic variable.
