determinants

The determinants of order $n$ is a number valued function of an ordered list of $n$ vectors $V_1,V_2 ,\dots ,V_n$ each with $n$ components. We denotes it as
$$
det(V_1,V_2,\dots, V_{n})
$$

  1. $det(V_1,V_2,\dots,V_n)$ is a multilinear function, that is , a linear function for each $V_i$, when all other $V_j, j \ne i$ are held fixed.
  2. If two vectors $V_i$ and $V_j$ of the ordered list are equal, the value of the dorminants is zero.
  3. $det(E_1,E_2, \dots , E_n)=1$, where $E_{j}$ is the vector whose $i-th$ component is 1 and all other components are zero.
  4. If two of vectors in the ordered set are interchanged, the value of the determinant is multiplied by $-1$.
  5. If $V_1,V_2,V_3 \dots,V_n$ are lineearly dependent, then $det(V_1,V_2,\dots V_{n})=0$
  6. If $V_1,V_2,V_3 \dots,V_n$ are lineearly dependent, then $det(V_1,V_2,\dots V_{n}) \ne0$

Proof of $4,5,6$:
$$
det(U+V,U+V)=det(U,U+V)+det(V,U+V)
$$

$$
=det(U,U)+det(U+V)+det(V,U)+det(V,V)
$$

$$
det(U,V)+det(V,U)=0
$$

If $V_1,V_2,V_3 \dots,V_n$ are linearly dependent, then
$$
V_1=\sum_{i=2}^{n}a_iV_i
$$

$$
det(V_1,V_2,\dots,V_n)=\sum_{i=3}^{n}det(a_iV_i,V_2,\dots,a_iV_i,\dots,V_n)+det(a_2V_2,a_2V_2,\dots,V_n)=0
$$

If $V_1,V_2,V_3 \dots,V_n$ are linearly dependent, then the unit coordinate vector $E_i$ can be expressed:
$$
E_i=\sum_{j=1}^{n}b_{ji}V_{j}
$$

$$
det(E_1,E_2,\dots,E_n)=det(\sum_{j=1}^{n}b_{j1}V_{j},E_2,\dots,E_n)
$$

$$
=\sum_{j=1}^{n}b_{j1} det(V_j,E_2,\dots,E_n)
$$

$$
=\sum_{j=1}^{n}b_{j1}\sum_{k=1}^{n}b_{k2} det(V_j,V_k,\dots,E_n)
$$

Continuing this we can get the expression
$$
det(E_1,E_2,\dots,E_n)=\sum_{permutation jk\dots z}(b_{j1}b_{k2}\dots b_{zn})det(V_j,V_k,\dots V_z)
$$
For each permutation, either
$$
det(V_j,V_k,\dots V_z)=-det(V_1,V_2,\dots,V_n)
$$
or
$$
det(V_j,V_k,\dots V_z)=det(V_1,V_2,\dots,V_n)
$$

$$
det(E_1,E_2,\dots,E_n)=1
$$

this prove (6)

Next we define the signature of permutation as follows. Donated by
$$
p=p_1 p_2\dots p_n
$$
Definition:

We define the sinature of a permutation $p$ of $1,2,\dots,n$ to be the nnumber $s(p)=1 $ or $-1$, such that
$$
\prod_{i<j}(X_{p_{i}}-x_{p_{j}})=s(p)\prod_{i<j}(x_i-x_j)
$$
$s(p)$ has following properties:

  1. The siganture of an intechange is $-1$.
  2. The signature of the composite of two permutation is the profuct is the product of the signatures $s(pq)=s(p)s(q)$

Proof:

The composite of $p$ and $q=q_1q_2\dots q_n$ can be expressed as
$$
pq=p_{q_{1}}p_{q_{2}}\dots p_{q_{n}}
$$

$$
\prod_{i<j}(x_{p_{q_{i}}}-x_{p_{q_{j}}})=\prod_{i<j}{(y_{q_i}-y_{q_j})}=s(q)\prod_{i<j}{(y_{i}-y_{j})}=s(p)s(q)\prod_{i<j}{(x_{i}-x_{j})}
$$

if we denote $x_{p_k}=y_k$ for every $k$

Definition:

Let $V_1,\dots,V_n$ be vectors in $R^n$ written as columns,

The determinants is defined as
$$
det(V_1,V_2,\dots,V_n)=\sum_{p}s(p)v_{p1}v_{p2}\dots v_{pn}
$$
Example:
$$
V_1=\begin{bmatrix}
v_{11} \ v_{21} \end{bmatrix}
V_2=\begin{bmatrix}
v_{12} \ v_{22} \end{bmatrix}
$$
$s(12)=1$

$s(21)=-1$

$det(V_1,V_2)=v_{11}v_{22}-v_{21}v_{12}$
